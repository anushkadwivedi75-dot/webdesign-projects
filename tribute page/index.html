About Ada Lovelace

Born in 1815, Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer. She is chiefly known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Her notes on the engine include what is recognized as the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine. This has earned her the title of the "first computer programmer."

Ada foresaw the potential of computers to go beyond mere number-crunching, envisioning them as tools that could manipulate symbols and even create music.
